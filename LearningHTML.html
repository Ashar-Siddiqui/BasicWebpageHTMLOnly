Our School

Santa Clara High School was established in 1872 and originally situated in the downtown area. It was moved to its present location in 1981 as a senior high school with grades 10-12. In the fall of 1987, the 9th grade was added and the school is presently a traditional 9-12. The school serves students from Santa Clara and San Jose. The campus is located on 31.7 acres. A new football field, new track, new tennis courts and other fields have been added, as well as a Science building. A new Performing Arts building is used frequently for local school productions. The new administration building and the renovated library are light, airy places in which to work. The main building was renovated and features comfortable and technologically advanced classrooms.


Our Teacher

Brian Van Dyck is a Computer Science teacher at Santa Clara High School, former Faculty CalStateTEACH CSU. He was one of the first hundred Google Certified Teachers, running the Computing and Programming Experience (CAPE) program. Mr. Van Dyck, along with CAPE, introduced the concepts of computer science to incoming high school freshmen. He was formerly the Embark Labs Director of Curriculum. He currently teaches our Intro to Computer Science class.


Our Exploring Computer Science Class

This year-long course explores the foundational concepts of computer science in a project-based learning environment. Topics include: History of Computing, Binary Concepts, Foundations of Programming, Block Programming plat- forms: Blockly and SCRATCH, Paper Prototyping and the Engineering/Design Process, Application Development uti- lizing MIT App Inventor, Robotics platforms: Lego Mind- storm, Dot n Dash, Sphero, MindBlock, and Ozobot, Intro- duction to Python and Introduction to HTML Web Design. Students will develop skills in the areas of Critical Thinking, Problem Solving, Design Thinking, Computing, and Pro- gramming.
